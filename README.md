# Bright-Coffe-Shop

#1. Project Overview
The goal of this project was to identify revenue drivers and operational inefficiencies for Bright Coffee Shop. We transitioned raw transactional data into a structured cloud environment to perform time-series analysis and product performance ranking.

#2. Tech Stack
*Miro: Used for "Logic Mapping" and architecting the data flow and entity relationships.
*Project planning: Canva (Gannt chat)
*Databricks: The primary engine for Big Data processing (SQL) is data cleaning
*Processing & ETL: Databricks (SQL)
*Analysis & Visualization:Google Sheet(Pivot Tables & Dashboards)
*Canva: Used to design the Executive Presentation, transforming charts into a high-end "CEO-Ready" visual story.
*Documentation: GitHub

#Key Insights & Findings
*The "Morning Peak" Dominance: Analysis revealed that 45% of revenue is generated between 06:00 and 11:00 AM.
*Category Leaders: Coffee and Tea represent ~70% of total volume, but Bakery items (15%) show a significant opportunity for "bundle" upselling.
*Store Performance: Astoria is the most consistent revenue generator, while Hell's Kitchen showed the highest growth rate in Q2 2026.
*The February Slump: Sales dipped in February due to seasonal trends; however, the business successfully recovered by March, entering a high-growth trajectory leading into summer.

#Summary of Actions
*Based on the data, I proposed three primary strategic actions to the CEO:
*Operational Optimization: Align barista staffing levels specifically with the "Morning Peak" identified in the SQL time-phase analysis.
*Growth Marketing: Launch a "Monday Blues" loyalty promotion to boost the week's slowest revenue day.
*Inventory Strategy: Prioritize stock for high-volume items in June (Peak Month) to prevent stock-outs and lost revenue.
